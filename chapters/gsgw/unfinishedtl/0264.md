# Chapter 263

After she got separated from the group while boarding the train, we finally found Assistant Manager Lee Seonghae at this casino station.

We could have been happy that the search had succeeded.

If she hadn't been working as a dealer in a casino ghost story with one eye missing.

"Assistant Ma---"

"Shh."

Agent Choi grabbed my shoulder tightly. I reflexively shut my mouth.

"......"

"Yes, Ms. Dealer. Please guide us~"

"Yesyes!"

And so, we quietly began moving, following the ghost story resident who looked like Assistant Manager Lee Seonghae, the dealer who had started walking ahead.

Agent Choi whispered in a low voice.

"Rather than causing a scene asking if she recognizes us, we need to find the perfect timing and snap her back to her senses, even if only briefly. Got it?"

I nodded.

The dealer guided us inward, passing through the run-down casino entrance area.

We entered while quietly observing our surroundings.

...If this were the <Dark Exploration Records>, the environment that appeared would surely have been recorded like this...

Upon passing through the casino entrance area under the dealer's guidance, a space appears in which three iron doors are aligned side by side, resembling a repurposed subway maintenance facility.

The areas accessible through each door are described below in order.

1. Left -- Red door

"This is the slot machine zone!"

When the door opened, a square space appeared, filled with fast-tempo, low-quality instrumental music, old brass decorations, and endlessly lined rows of slot machines.

Compared to the decorative machines at the entrance, this place felt more systematic and persistent.

"You can enjoy a simple game by pulling the lever, and you can challenge the jackpot with just one coin, so it's very popularr."

Most of the slot machines were already broken, but there were countless visitors blankly pulling the levers of the shabby machines that were still operating.

If they had no hands, they used their feet. If they had no feet, they used their mouths.

I even saw someone who had just won desperately grabbing whatever came out of a winning slot machine and fleeing to a corner.

And addicts chasing that winner like mad.

It was a hair-raising sight.

"Then I'll guide you to the next areaa."

2. Right -- Blue door

"The dealer zone. This is where various table games are played. Only parties holding 50 coins or more may enterr."

Compared to the slot machine zone, a somewhat cleaner casino space was revealed.

However, more than half of the game tables were broken or shattered.

At the few tables that were still intact, 'dealers' dressed similarly to Assistant Manager Lee Seonghae stood motionless or were running games.

But their numbers were noticeably small, and there were hardly any seated visitors...

[It feels isolated. Oh, like a run-down casino at a tourist destination no one visits anymore.]

[Look at those people. Such shabby appearances!]

The visitors sitting in front of the dealers either had no legs, or were missing more than half of their facial features, or had their entire bodies covered in worn-out hoodies or padded jackets.

"......"

"How would you like to proceedd?"

"Ah, it seems like today's a rather quiet day."

"People usually visit more on weekends than weekdayss!"

It clearly wasn't that kind of issue, but it was only natural that realistic conversation was difficult with contaminated people.

I gently changed the subject.

"Actually, I was a bit surprised to meet a dealer. I thought this was an unmanned facility."

"Ah. I understandd. I heard that since subway ridership has been declining recently, ■■ City Royal Casino has also been changing its management policy in a new and more innovative directionn."

It was a loaded statement.

The dealer wearing Assistant Manager Lee Seonghae's face smiled faintly and guided us to the next room.

"But there's also a special game you can only enjoy heree!"

The final room.

3. Center -- Gold door

A rusted iron door with its gold paint completely peeled away.

Above it hung a velvet nameplate that had not yet frayed.

@rs@*VIP ACCESS*@rs@

"Only visitors holding 999 coins or more may enter this roomm!"

Just as she said, the iron door was firmly shut.

As if we were not allowed inside.

"This is a zone I'd strongly recommend, where you can witness the true form of Royal Casino."

"What kind of game is played there?"

"That is something I can only tell visitors who meet the entry requirements."

"How many people are inside right now?"

"That is something I can only tell visitors who meet the entry requirements."

In other words, if we wanted to know anything at all about the VIP room behind that door, we needed 999 coins.

"What I can say for sure is that it's possible to earn an enormous amount of coins!"

"...I see."

[Hmm. Are you curious, *Friend*?]

Of course, it felt ominous, but of course, that wasn't what mattered right now.

"Then allow me to usher you into the dealer room firstt!"

She had definitely been more than 10 centimeters shorter than Assistant Manager Eun Haje, but now, even at a glance, she was taller.

And she wasn't even wearing cushioned sneakers like she usually did, just plain dress shoes.

How was that possible?

"Would you like to play a gamee?"

Back in the second area, the dealer room, the dealer moved to the opposite side of a shabby table and explained in a lively voice.

"It's a simple card game. You just have to guess the identity of the card I'm holding! You can bet a minimum of 3 coins, and you can earn up to 36 times your bet!"

Assistant Manager Lee Seonghae's hands, as she pulled out a deck of playing cards, moved deftly.

And I realized something else.

"Would you like to place a bet?"

The dealer with one eye met the gaze of me, who also had one eye.

"If you bet all 35 coins you're holding, then in theory, you could obtain VIP room access in a single game."

......

"That's alright."

I smiled and took a small step back from the table.

"It's our first time here. We'll try the slot machines first, and if possible, enjoy ourselves a bit before coming back to bet. Thank you."

"...Yesyes. Thank you!"

The dealer's persistent gaze finally lifted, and I left the dealer room with the others.

Only after we returned to the entrance area and confirmed that the dealer wasn't following us near the pawnshop did I speak.

"Grapes-ie, did you notice?"

"Yes."

I had seen the dealer's hands while she shuffled the cards.

"Only the ring finger and middle finger had different shapes."

"Right. And the wrist and the hand too, the bone connections looked unnatural."

And even the dealer's height, which was strangely tall compared to Assistant Manager Lee Seonghae.

All of it pointed to a single fact.

......

"Only the head is Assistant Manager Dolphin."

The dealer was a crude assembly of different body parts.

With Assistant Manager Lee Seonghae's head attached.

"She kept gambling here, repeatedly selling most of her body parts and then buying them back."

Or.

"She might have gone into debt and lost her entire body to the casino, then got reassembled as a dealer."

Either way, one question remained.

So.

"...Can we consider her to be in a state that can still be rescued?"

......

"We have to shoot her," Agent Bronze spoke up.

"Wait---"

But he insisted again.

"Then she'll wake up in reality. Just shoot her in the head and that's it."

"Wait!"

Assistant Manager Eun Haje cut him off.

"Sir Highborn Civil Servant. Calm down for a second. Let's think about it. ...Can someone with only a head really be considered themselves?"

"Hmm~ The side with the brain should count as the self, shouldn't it? After all, the one dreaming would be the head."

"No."

Agent Choi and Section Chief Lee Jaheon disagreed.

The two looked at each other.

"Mr. Civilian, could you explain why you think that?"

"Yes. In Darknesses, there are cases where symbolic or ritual classifications are used instead of medical diagnoses."

"...So you're saying the basis might not be the head but the heart? Since there are cultures that see the heart as a more fundamental element of a person?"

"Yes."

"Huu."

Agent Choi fell into a low hum.

If it's not recognized that 'Assistant Manager Lee Seonghae with only a head left' was the real person, and she didn't wake up in reality...

'Her consciousness might just die as it is.'

And on top of that.

"Even here, there's no guarantee like at the previous station that 'if you die, you'll immediately wake up in reality,' Sir Highborn Geezer."

"...Even so, that option is more humane than leaving her behind in a state of complete contamination inside an unperceived annihilation-sanctioned supernatural disaster."

"Bronze-ah."

Agent Choi's low voice was heard.

"I told you not to quote the bureau manual like it's your personal opinion. Right?"

"......"

"My hoobae here is a bit rigid, that's all. He didn't mean any harm by it, dear civilians."

"I know. Well, what's been said does have a bit of a hasty generalization problem, but it's not entirely wrong either."

Assistant Manager Eun Haje softened her words, as if calming things down.

"We brought euthanasia drugs with us because we figured that if there was no other way out, death was the only option anyway."

That was true.

'I did assume the gimmick probably hadn't changed drastically.'

And besides.

"I think so too. ...Given that I'm in human form, it's even clearer that we entered a dream, that this isn't reality."

"...Roe."

"So if there really is no other method, we may have to do what Agent Bronze suggested. But for now, I'd like to look into other options first."

"......"

Agent Bronze nodded heavily. The others seemed to have reached a point of agreement as well.

Gather as much information as possible inside the Midnight Station Casino of Corporeity.

That was our first objective.

That said, there was someone who clearly didn't agree.

Baek Saheon, with a face that looked like it had 'not interested' written all over it.

He didn't have much personal connection to Assistant Manager Lee Seonghae to begin with, and since the one paying the reward was Ho Yoowon, rescue wasn't his goal.

'He's probably more interested in the payoff.'

And Ho Yoowon had made an unusually generous request this time.

-- For now, I'd like you to gather information about Sekwang City outside the subway.

So rather than leaving blindly, other related information could be obtained by thoroughly exploring the stations.

And even if that wasn't the case, a casino is the perfect place to obtain items.

'He has his mind on other things.'

Perhaps noticing that uncooperative air, Agent Bronze shot Baek Saheon a resentful look from behind his mask.

But that's not how you handle this.

I grabbed Baek Saheon by the shoulder.

"Hey."

"Y-Yes, sir?"

Baek Saheon (fortunately) flinched. But this time, it was a carrot rather than a stick.

I spoke as gently as possible.

"If this is a casino, wouldn't the dealer know the mechanisms and products here better than anyone?"

"Then?"

"I'm saying that if we rescue her and bring her with us, we could get some pretty valuable information."

"......"

Perhaps he had already gathered information about Assistant Manager Lee Seonghae's personality, because Baek Saheon's expression shifted slightly.

Right. He knows she isn't the type to take help and then skip out without paying it back.

"Think about it."

I let go of him.

'That should be enough to at least keep him from interfering.'

With that, everyone split up.

Assistant Manager Eun Haje went to observe other casino visitors, Section Chief Lee Jaheon and Baek Saheon headed to the slot machines, and the agents dispersed to check the body pawnshop and item exchange counters.

And as for me...

"Welcome back!"

I entered the dealer room.

-- Assistant Manager, could you lend me 20 coins for a moment? I won't spend them. I just want to enter the dealer room.

-- Okay. I'll head over soon too, so come out to meet me.

Carefully borrowing those 'blood-like' coins from Assistant Manager Eun Haje, I met the 50-coin requirement and gained the right to play games with the dealer.

"Yes. We meet again."

I sat across the table.

The dealer wearing Assistant Manager Lee Seonghae's face smiled brightly.

"Would you like to place a bett?"

"...Yes."

"Alright!"

"I'll give you a heads-up first. If you're caught using 'cheats', a fine will be imposed."

"Yes. I'll be careful."

As I carefully observed every movement, I spoke casually, as if in passing.

"Have you ever worked somewhere else before?"

"...Pardon?"

"I think I saw you at a resort."

The hand moving the cards paused for a moment.

And then...

"Ah, I worked briefly at Flower Golden Resortt. It was a really nice place!"

"...I see. That's great."

"Thank youu."

So at least she remembered that much.

I decided to proceed with the game, both to move toward more personal, memory-triggering questions and to learn what Assistant Manager Lee Seonghae had gone through.

Just then.

"Then let's start the ga--- oh!"

'Who is it?'

"...!"

"......"

"......"

'What the?'

I had a bad feeling.

I considered standing up, but for now, I stayed seated.

Cold sweat formed in my palm.

"Would you like to place a bet?"

Stained, worn coins.

"......"

In contrast, my clean coins glinted faintly in the light...

"Yesyes. Then we'll begin!"

The dealer collected the coins, spread the deck of playing cards face down on the table, and asked.

"Which card would you like to choose?"

I waited.

But the one seated beside me didn't choose a card.

A suffocating silence.

"...This one."

"Yesyess!"

'...What?'

Something felt off.

[Oh, those odds only benefit the casino. How blatantly obvious.]

Yeah.

"So it's a probability game. The multiplier for numbers is higher than for suits, so it seems like it's ranked higher. Like a precious solution."

"That's an interesting comparison, dear guest!"

"Then have you ever played the game as a visitor yourself, Ms. Dealer? I was wondering how many coins you've wagered at most."

"I've bet up to 990 coins."
