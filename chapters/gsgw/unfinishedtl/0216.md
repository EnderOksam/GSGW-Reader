# Chapter 215

The Security Team members looked up.

A dark pantry.

From beneath the shadows, the sales representative had fallen to the floor and was holding his breath. At that moment, he locked eyes with them as they emerged through portable transport equipment.

"Little one...... no."

The one wearing an animal mask drawn like a child's crayon drawing, a badger, suppressed his own triggered contamination.

Soon, a calm and businesslike voice followed.

"Employee from Sales Team 4."

"Y-Yes..."

"Identity confirmed. We will now begin calculating the fee."

The sales rep swallowed.

Clad head-to-toe in black uniforms, faces covered, equipped with all sorts of strange devices, they themselves were like living ghost stories.

Supernatural, unmistakably assigned to special duties. Yet, for a moment, their presence brought a sense of relief.

Of course, the very next moment, the incomprehensible situation flooded him with fear again.

"Um, so, what am I supposed to do...?"

As he rolled his eyes, trying desperately to grasp the situation, the sales rep spotted something.

The Security Team member who followed was carrying a black portable carrier in his hand.

A metal carrier, seemingly for animal transport.

...Wait a minute.

"Y-You're not going to put me in there and take me out, are you...?"

"No. Of course not."

Badger gently patted the carrier.

It was a moment of relief for the sales rep.

"There's already someone inside."

"......Huh?"

"Don't think too much about it."

The sales rep, who seemed half-crazed by this incomprehensible situation, looked on as Badger gave a bittersweet smile inwardly.

And then...

"It's cramped..."

Holding the carrier in one hand, J3 seemed to have little interest in the situation itself.

Of course, it had been a very long time since he'd been 'out' for 'work' not wearing his security guard uniform.

Though calling it 'out' was a stretch, considering how cramped their current location was.

In the meantime, Badger displayed an absurd level of courtesy for a Security Team member like himself. He announced in a businesslike tone,

"All received details have been confirmed. We will now proceed to..."

"Who is it?"

"Who's there?"

"...!"

*Tadadadadadak.*

A sound like raindrops hitting the door gnawed at their nerves.

Fingernails were scraping.

Ten fingertips scratched at the door.

"There's someone else."

"There's more."

The voices whispered, as if gasping for breath.

The sound was terrifyingly close. Because...

They were pressing their heads to the gap in the door, whispering through it.

"Too late."

"It's too late."

The couple was peering inside.

Into the very room they themselves had locked.

The sales rep felt frozen to the marrow of his bones.

But...

"Are they human?"

"Can't say..."

The astonishingly calm conversation of the 'Security Team' came from right beside him.

"Maybe... they used to be?"

Out of habit, J3 reached to adjust his nonexistent security cap, and instead patted his mask.

Something like the faded eyes of a beast flashed over it.

Then he carefully set the carrier down on the pantry floor.

Right next to the sales rep.

"Um... Can you wait here...?"

"Y, Yes?"

But it wasn't directed at the sales rep.

"You alright...?"

*...Tap.*

The iron bars of the black carrier rattled softly.

As if the animal inside was responding.

"I'll be... right back. Wait here......"

*Tap.*

Another response came.

J3 gently touched the carrier, then straightened up.

"Um... if you don't want to get hurt... stick close to this side, over here......"

This time, the words were for the sales rep.

"Absolutely... do not handle it carelessly."

"......"

The sales rep swallowed and nodded. And he pressed himself to the side of the black carrier.

Sensing that this was a form of protection.

...A strange chill radiated from within the carrier.

"Then, we will begin."

Leaving the sales rep behind, the two Security Team members moved to the pantry door and opened it with force.

"...!!"

Their bodies blocked the view, so he couldn't see outside, but he heard the rain get even louder. The sales rep shrank himself as small as possible. He caught a glimpse of the Security Team members heading outside...

The door closed, and the sound of the rain faded.

*Thud.*

......

*Screeeeech, screeech, scritch.*

Something was being ripped apart, the noise was thunderous.

The sales rep, without realizing, gripped the carrier tightly. He did it to avoid imagining what was happening outside.

The cold kept him alert.

Inside the carrier, something like strange smoke seemed to slither up and rise...

After a single moment that felt endlessly long.

*BOOM.*

"...!"

The roaring stopped, and even the rain ceased.

The door opened.

And there were the two Security Team members, standing as if nothing had happened, checking their equipment...

"Suppression complete."

"......!"

They're fine.

A faint hope rose in the sales rep's heart, nearly causing his legs to give out.

Then... can he leave now? The employee got to his feet and looked out the door.

And in that instant, he understood what 'suppression' meant.

"Ah--- mmf."

He suppressed a scream.

The hallway was slick with moisture.

The wallpaper and floor were soaked, shining with a dark, clammy reflection like spattered bodily fluids, almost like blood.

And directly opposite the door.

There was the bizarre form of the couple, their bodies twisted and fixed, all their limbs bent backward and locked in place.

"...!"

As if determined to break and crush every possible means of movement, to thoroughly prevent any resistance, the two were twisted into monstrous, centipede-like shapes, each joint pinned in place with colored push pins, like oversized tacks.

Overwhelming force and violence.

And alongside that, childish school supplies.

From every hole in their faces, fluid was running down, as if displayed on the dark hallway wall.

Just like his dead superior.

No, even more inhuman.

"......"

"Ah... right. It was a collection job."

Collection?

"Starting with information gathering?"

"Mmh..."

The security team member, gaunt and slow-moving, approached what had once been the 'couple', whom he had smashed and pinned in place...

He pulled a push pin from the mouth.

"......!"

"Hey... is there... a child here?"

It was one of the things the sales rep had said into the pager.

A chill ran through his chest.

Even more horrifying, the couple's mouth opened and began to answer.

"P-Please, help us. Please, help... *hicc."*

"Answer the question first."

Suddenly.

Tears streamed from their eyes.

"......!"

"The child, the child is in the bathroom."

"That poor child. Please, please help..."

"You're already trapped, too. It's too late... please help..."

"......"

The security team member called 'sergeant' just stared at them.

"What's upstairs?"

"There's something evil. Don't go up. No, you need to run away from here."

"Maybe you can still escape. Somehow, to the outside..."

"Hmm......"

Pondering, the security team member raised a hand toward them.

And in the next moment.

"...Enough."

*...Snap.*

With a sound as clear as it was final, the couple's necks were broken.

The skinny security officer, with one hand on each neck, crushed and twisted the bones with such bizarre strength, spinning them backward.

With total nonchalance.

......

Did he just.

......

Kill them?

"Ah, we've confirmed everything we needed, then."

"Yeah."

"Understood. ...Mr. Caller, you should be all right now. Please hand over what you're holding."

His hands trembled.

Something was terribly wrong.

No human could do something like that...

"Mr. Caller?"

The sales rep forced himself to look down at his own hand.

He was holding the sketchbook paper.

And on it, new words appeared.

iM scAr*ed*

Truly.

mOns*Ter*

Suddenly.

The sales rep turned his head.

To the spot right beside the pantry.

The answer the paper had been hinting at all along.

...The place even the dead couple had mentioned.

bAth*RO*om *tub*

The bathroom.

The sales rep ran---

"No can do."

"...!!"

His head was seized.

Gasping for air, the sales rep looked up and saw, covering his face, a grotesque and enormous shape.

A beast's forepaw with countless claws.

"Stay... right here......"

The sales rep blinked. The fear of death left him paralyzed...

He's going to die.

"Teacher, let go of what's in your hand! Listen to the rules your colleague tells you. That's how you can be the perfect teacher!"

His hand loosened. The sketchbook paper slipped away.

No.

"Should I read it...?"

"No. You'll get contaminated if you read it, so later..."

The sales rep moved his eyes, the only thing he could control. Run. Where should he run. Front door, living room, upstairs, window...

'Ah.'

Right.

He could escape through the window!

Why didn't he think of that? Yes, if he died going out the front, he could go out the window. He had to escape, escape right now... now!

He struggled, trying to crawl to the window, kicking, pushing at the beast's paw with his nails, but nothing worked. He couldn't give u---

"If you go there... you'll die."

......

Huh?

"Just stay still..."

"D, Die? Why..."

"Mm. Because... you get closer to the source...?"

The source?

He turned his head.

"You should remember. You said it yourself, Mr. Caller."

What?

"When you reported, you said it... this house felt strange now and then."

"......"

The employee stopped in his tracks.

He remembered what he'd yelled into the pager, the desperate plea for help.

What his superior had said.

"The rain..."

*-- That house feels a little off, especially when it rains.*

The sales rep looked at the window.

Outside, the rain fell in a steady drizzle...

......

"On rainy days... That's right......"

The slow, apathetic voice crept chillingly into his ear.

"They say if you open a black umbrella inside the house while it's still wet, something will come in... If you leave wet shoes upside down, a ghost will wear them..."

"Sergeant, is that true?"

"No......"

The sergeant glanced at Badger with eyes devoid of reflection, then continued in a slow voice.

"Most of it's just... nonsense......"

"Oh."

"But... sometimes...... they say things like that can actually cause it to happen......"

Darkness.

The employee, who had once been a supervisor on the Field Exploration Team, filled in the word the sergeant didn't bother to add.

The sergeant's explanation wasn't finished.

"And... you know there's that other story......"

"......"

"When you're walking with an umbrella in the rain, and you hear a strange whisper, a voice talking to you from somewhere beyond... Don't answer..."

......

"Because it's not human..."

The sales rep met the sergeant's eyes.

"Hey, did anyone keep trying to talk to you......?"

At that moment.

"Supervisor Lee?"

The sales rep froze.

"Supervisor Lee, are you there?"

A familiar voice came from the front door.

*Knock, knock, knock.*

Someone was knocking on the door.

"Supervisor Lee, open the door, will you? What's going on in there?"

It was...

"Why aren't you coming out?"

The voice of his dead superior.

*Knock, knock, knock, knock.*

The body of the assistant manager, whose neck had been twisted and left in the entryway, had vanished.

Now, only the sound of someone knocking on the door from the other side echoed beyond the rain...

Accompanied by the assistant manager's voice.

"Open this up. You need to come out, too."

All strength left the sales rep's body.

The sergeant released his grip on the employee's head. With a thud, the sales rep collapsed to the floor, looking dazed.

'Wow...'

Badger watched this, quietly impressed.

He realized the sergeant's expertise in exploring Darknesses, concealed behind that lifeless manner of speaking.

It was the kind of skill the clever youngest member of the D-squad might have gained after a few more years on the Field Exploration Team...

...No, well. Since they were both now assigned to the Security Team, it was a meaningless thought. Badger caught himself, thinking he'd almost been rude.

Meanwhile, the voices multiplied.

"People! There's people here. Are you alright?"

"Aigoo. This old woman here fell down... Could you help me?"

"...Wait a minute, the grandmother of this house died years ago... Don't listen. Something's wrong here!"

Upstairs, the master bedroom, past the kitchen door...

Voices came from every direction, as if everyone who had ever been in the house had suddenly regained consciousness after the blackout.

They drew closer and closer...

Along with the sound of rain.

"Where are you?"

"...! Don't answer the voice from upstairs. There's no one up there right now...!"

"Be careful. I think there's something wrong here..."

Footsteps.

And then.

"Ah, the door opened."

......

"I'm coming. Where are you?"

Even the dead assistant manager.

"......Ugh, *huff."*

What is this? What's real?

Which of these is the real monster?

The sales rep tried to review what he'd heard to sort it out, but Badger grabbed him.

"Ack!"

"Calm down. It's alrigh--- Sergeant?"

In a situation that would make anyone's heart race with terror, the sergeant calmly knelt down and spoke into the transport cage.

He addressed whatever was inside the cage.

"What do you think... Does it sound right...?"

*Tap.*

"Mm. Then... should I do it......"

*Tap, tap.*

"......Still."

*Tap, tap.*

"......"

The sergeant fiddled with his mask and reluctantly shuffled aside.

A single tap meant yes, two taps meant no.

Seeing this agreed-upon signal, Badger gave a wry smile beneath his masked face.

Really, nothing had changed.
