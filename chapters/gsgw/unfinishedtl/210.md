# Episode 209

At 6 p.m., when the sun begins to set.

The dim glow of the sunset reflects on the entrance of a building in Seoul. A cold and pristine first-floor space with a modern structure. Annex of Daydream Inc.

This building restricts entry to authorized personnel only. This is the lobby of the annex of Daydream Inc.

However, this desolate space, which should have been quiet and eerie, was now buzzing with the lively chatter of people. 

“Wow, what an atmosphere.” 

“Hello. Yes, yes. Nice to meet you…”

Dozens of new employees in black suits were gathered, greeting each other. Their faces were bright with anticipation and nervousness. It was only natural. 

“Congratulations on joining!”

“Haha, thank you. You too!”

They had just succeeded in joining Daydream, a renowned pharmaceutical company that had been a hot topic in the stock market in recent years!

Thus, the company had gathered them at the annex next to the Seoul headquarters via email. New Employee Orientation Notice Today was the first day of orientation. 

“A three-day, two-night orientation at the Seoul annex? That’s kind of extravagant, isn’t it?”

“Totally. Wow, this place feels like the lobby of an art gallery.”

“Man, as expected of a big corporation… Wait, is everyone here a sales employee?”

“It seems like it. From what I’ve heard, everyone’s in sales.”

“Then maybe new hires from other departments are gathered somewhere nearby.” 

People, elated with the joy of being hired, laughed and chatted with each other. There was no reason not to be amicable. These were their fellow new hires, the victorious ones who had secured jobs at this pharmaceutical giant, and they would see each other often from now on!

Naturally, some even extended kindness to those who seemed left out of the conversations. 

“Hey, you’re a new hire too, right?”

“…”

Among the noisy crowd of new employees, someone who had been quietly staring at the interior of the annex looked up. 

It was a peer with a pale, hollow complexion.

So, the new hire who had been leading the conversation continued speaking to him, a bit boastfully.

“Congratulations on joining! Let’s do well together!”

But…

“Thank you.”

That was it.

“…”

“…”

Silence.

What’s with this guy?

How could a new sales employee be that unapproachable? How did he even pass the interview?

As the atmosphere threatened to turn awkward, the new hire who had initiated the conversation hurriedly rambled on. 

“Haha, actually… one of my friends already works at Daydream.” 

“Wow.”

“For real?”

“Whoa, what department?”

“Yeah, I think they said the research team?”

The reaction from the others was explosive!

The new hire, secretly relieved at reviving the mood, continued in a subtly boastful tone. “But get this—they kept telling me not to come here. Said I’d regret it or something.” 

“Haha!”

“That’s what friends who got hired first always say.”

“Exactly!”

“…” 

People laughed as if they were hearing the usual complaints about workplace overwork. But… 

“…Department.”

“Huh?”

The silent person spoke up.

“That friend of yours… what department were they in?”

At that moment.

[Ahem, mic test.]

“…!”

Everyone looked up at once.

[Whoa, looks like everyone’s gathered nicely. Let’s get started, then~]

A speaker installed in the corner of the wall.

A lively, cheerful male voice began to emanate from it. 

A smooth-talking man’s voice.

[We welcome all the new employees who made it through the 142:1 competition! I’m Kwak Je-kang, the manager of Research Team 1, and I’ll be leading your orientation today.]

[Shall we give a round of applause to kick things off?!]

Waaa!

The new employees clapped enthusiastically, though they were slightly puzzled. “Why over the speaker…?”

Why wasn’t he showing himself in person?

“Maybe it’s something we’re doing among ourselves?”

[Bingo! That’s correct!] 

“…!”

He’s listening…!

[That’s right, that’s right. From now on, to build teamwork and company loyalty, you new hires will participate in some very fun activities together~]

[It’s just a light mission, basically a recreation activity. And there are prizes at stake!!]

The new employees quickly took it in stride.

“Guess they want us to bond quickly!”

“That’s actually pretty cool. Haha.”

They’d heard that big corporations or famous startups often hold highly creative or unique orientations these days.

“I heard some companies even do murder mystery board games with the company as the setting.” 

[…Haha, that sounds fun. This is similar. Yes, indeed~ Alright, alright…]

The voice from the speaker grew excited, its tone rising.

[Let’s officially begin the new employee orientation!!]

At the same time.

Click-click-click.

The lights in the annex entrance turned on.

“…!” Under the bright incandescent lights, things that weren’t visible before came into view. Over there, heavy iron doors lined a dark corridor.

“…”

They didn’t seem like they were for security so much as for isolating or trapping something… heavy and alien.

Why would a company annex have those?

But before they could dwell on the question, their attention was drawn to the brightest spot under the lights.

A reception desk, standing alone.

On it, a conspicuous object.

“Oh…!”

The new employees blinked and examined it.

A black cylindrical box.

“A lottery box?”

It was large and worn.

It looked out of place in the modern, pristine marble lobby of the annex, tattered and crumpled. The thick paper lottery box was covered so its contents couldn’t be seen, but the silhouette of the lid revealed three holes.

Each circular lid was numbered.

(1) (2) (3) 

[Alright, one by one, come up and draw a lot. Take turns picking the next number, and keep going like that.]

[Oh, let’s show an example. The three people standing closest right now! Pick one number each in order!!]

“Oh, yes!”

Amid the murmurs, the three designated people hesitated briefly but stepped forward, unable to back out.

And they drew their lots.

Rustle. 

The contents of the lots were as follows:

(1) 6th basement floor

(2) Room 0322

(3) Recite a lullaby 

“This is…”

[Now, look at the desk again. See the whiteboard behind the lottery?]

Everyone’s eyes turned to the whiteboard.

A printed sentence was displayed.

I go down to basement (1) _____

At door (2) _____

I will (3) _____

[Fill in the blanks with the contents of your lots, and the sentence will be complete!!] 

[Your mission is to carry out that sentence. Simple, right? Haha!!]

As people filled in the whiteboard with the numbers… the sentence was completed. Like this: 

I go down to the 6th basement floor

At door 0322

Recite a lullaby.

“…”

“…”

The completed sentence gave off an inexplicably eerie feeling.

Basement?

At that moment.

[Oh, by the way, there’s an amazing prize waiting for the team with the best mission performance.]

As if sensing their unease, a cheerful, motivating voice came from the speaker.

[It’s 100 points that you can use like cash at the employee store!] 

A hundred points?

“What can you buy with that?”

[Of course! That’s enough to buy a robot vacuum cleaner!]

“Ohh!”

If it could buy a robot vacuum, it must be a significant amount!

The atmosphere brightened instantly.

Rationality and a sense of reality returned with the calculations.

At worst, it’s like a test of courage.

No way a company would make us do something actually dangerous.

Besides, with so many people and groups of three, it was hard to feel scared. Even if they’d prepared a jump scare, it would surely be something even the most timid could handle!

The new employees nodded and smiled at each other.

“Do we need to take the stairs?”

[Oh, that’d be too tiring, wouldn’t it? You can use any of the elevators currently running. Haha!!] 

[Alright, form your teams as you like and give it a try!!]

“Yes!”

They chatted excitedly, putting their hands into the black box to draw their lots. The lots that would decide their fate.

“Fighting!”

“Our group will go first. This elevator, right?”

“Let’s do this. 6th basement floor!”

The mood couldn’t have been better.

Until, 34 minutes later, the elevator returned, and its doors opened to reveal only the lower halves of their colleagues.

“Aaah!”

“Ahhhh!”

And it continued.

Ding.

Ding.

Ding.

The sound of the elevator arriving.

Each time the doors opened, the piercing screams of the people echoed through the annex lobby like a dissonant cacophony. 

Screams, terror, panic.

Every other elevator that arrived was filled with what used to be human bodies. Horrific sights unimaginable in reality—blood, filth, pieces of organs, sometimes ash and ice, or scraps of paper densely scribbled with phrases in pen.

Some employees had written grotesque phrases or symbols on the elevator mirrors with their own blood before dying of exsanguination.

“Aaaah!!!”

Even those who returned alive were out of their minds.

“We have to escape! Run! Run!”

“I’m sorry, I’m sorry. I’m sorry for knocking on the door…” Some mumbled incoherently, their minds gone.

Then, a few relatively sane people appeared.

“This person’s fine!”

“…”

“What the hell happened?!”

The new hire, who had been using a dead colleague’s body like a shield in front of the elevator door, spoke with a deathly pale face.

“There’s someone there.”

In the basement they’d gone to, beyond the door…

“Who?! Who was it?!”

“…An odd employee.”

And the survivors’ accounts poured out.

When the elevator doors opened, a silent, desolate basement corridor appeared. Numerous iron doors lined it, with a strange mark only on the door they were supposed to visit. Sounds, a fishy smell, flickering lights… and the view they glimpsed through the small hole above the door’s number plate.

The employee.

“They were… strange.”

A child-like figure, one in a doctor’s coat, one with an oxygen tank, one holding a bunch of balloons, one wearing a cowboy hat with wooden hands, one in a lion dance costume… The descriptions of their bizarre appearances sent chills down everyone’s spines. And then.

“They reacted.”

“…!”

“They reacted to what we did! They chased us, and… like that!” Heads turned toward the body parts filling the elevators. 

“…”

“…”

The new employees realized.

The mission to “follow the sentence” was a kind of horror movie suicide act. They were caught in an unreal catastrophe.

“No way!”

The remaining half of the new hires, who hadn’t yet gone down for their mission, looked at each other with ashen faces and rushed to the main entrance. But the annex’s only door was already sealed.

People clung to the shuttered door.

“Please, save us!”

“My phone’s not working. What do we do?”

“This… this has to be a prank show. Yeah, there’s got to be a camera somewhere…”

“Open the door! Please, open the door!” 

Bang bang bang!

No response came, even as they pounded the shutter until their hands bled. Some new hires noticed another way to communicate.

“The speaker!”

They rushed to the speakers where “Manager Kwak Je-kang’s” voice had come from, pouring out pleas for rescue, cries of fear, and desperation.

But…

[(Beep—) The person in charge is currently unavailable, so the automated response system has been activated.]

The voice of “Manager Kwak Je-kang” no longer came from the speakers. Instead, a cheerful recorded voice repeated. 

[The main entrance will open after all participants have attempted the mission. Thirty seconds from now, any attempt to forcibly open the shutter will result in death by security protocol.]

[Orientation participants, do your best!!]

Beep beep beep—Beep!

“Aaah!”

Two employees trying to break the shutter died as their heads exploded. Chaos.

“Wha—what…”

“Sob…”

No one could understand the logic or what was happening. Faced with a scene too unreal to believe, the commotion among the people gradually quieted.

From then on, the people in the lobby huddled together in front of the desk, trembling, for over half a day.

Occasional murmurs and movements to find a way out arose, but no one dared approach the elevators.

The mission showed no signs of proceeding.

Then, an astonishing incentive was given.

[Due to a delay in mission progress exceeding 6 hours, we are providing guidance.] 

[If the next participant does not board the elevator within 5 minutes, death by security protocol will occur.]

“…”

“…”

The new employees looked at each other with trembling eyes. Someone.

Had to be sent.

To the elevator.

Who would it be?

“Damn it…”

The new hire who had bragged about their friend working at the company tried to avoid the gazes, slinking into a corner.

Sweating coldly, they looked again at the sentence their group had completed.

I go down to the 13th basement floor

At door 666

Knock and enter.

They’d die.

There was no way they could survive with such ominous numbers.

Escape…

At that moment.

“The people who drew the next lots! Those people!”

Damn it!

“…!”

“There they are, it’s them!”

The crowd rushed forward, forcibly shoving their group into the elevator. They resisted frantically, but they were outnumbered. They had no weapons, either. “No!”

[The doors are closing.]

Just as they scrambled up in a panic, desperately trying to press the open button, someone from their group grabbed their hand and pulled it down.

“…!” 

“It’s too late.”

Thud.

The doors closed.

“Hic!”

Before they could even feel anger, they turned their head in terror and saw the pale-faced peer looking at them.

The oddly quiet one. But now, they spoke calmly, as if explaining. “With the security protocol, people died. So staying in the lobby would mean dying anyway.”

“…”

“Now that it’s come to this, we have to do it.”

“N-no…!”

“Calm down and think. Plenty of people came back fine.” As if to snap them out of it, the other person grabbed their shoulders.

“…Surely, about one in three teams said ‘nothing happened,’ right?”

“…!!”

It was true. 

—Nothing happened… 

—There was just… someone in a strange uniform, like a special agent, staring at us silently. 

—There was really no one inside!

“Even among the teams where something happened, half the people came back alive. So, our chances of survival are higher doing this than staying in the lobby and dying.”

“…”

“…Let’s move carefully based on the testimonies of those who went before us.”

Breathing heavily, the two new hires slowly nodded. 

It was thanks to the other’s almost experienced calmness. The boastful new hire couldn’t help but speak up.

“Uh, what’s your name…?”

“…”

The silent peer spoke.

“I’m Jang Heowoon.”

Ding.

The elevator arrived at the basement.

The new hires stepped tremblingly onto the old-fashioned corridor lined with iron doors.

And they found the room they were looking for.

666 

Taking a deep breath, they placed a hand on the door.

Knock knock knock. 

***

I had a dream.

A truly strange and eerie nightmare.

A dream where I entered the creepy ghost story wiki I loved. In the dream, I became an elite employee at a horrific pharmaceutical company that threw people into ghost stories to make ingredients for medicine. And I worked as a spy infiltrating a special organization, the Supernatural Disaster Management Agency, to save people from those ghost stories. The countless ghost stories from the Dark Exploration Records wiki I entered during that process. 

A convenience store where you play hide-and-seek with ghosts, a quiz show where you die if you don’t answer correctly, an ink painting haunted by window ghosts, an exhibition that collects organs as admission fees, a bizarre theme park…

Intense, chilling days teetering on the edge of life and death. And yet, I met unforgettable people along the way. 

—Mr. Kim Soleum. Can you hear me? 

—Roe Deer-ya, seventeen employees escaped thanks to your announcement!

—I hope your wish comes true.

—It’ll be okay, Grape.

A lingering afterimage remains. 

You know those dreams where, even if you forget most of the details upon waking, the vivid impressions and emotions hit you like a shock.

Especially when the ending is horrific and shocking.

I endured terror and survived deadly situations while working at the ghost story company, and the moment I finally consumed the wish-granting right I’d longed for…

The truth is, I didn’t crash-land in another world.

I was invited.

My body isn’t human.

…I realized…

No!

I don’t want to wake up.

I don’t want to know the truth.

I don’t want to realize that I’m still at Daydream Inc. …

And the next moment, I realized.

I cannot sleep. 

“Gasp.”

Dawn.

I sat up in bed, gasping for breath.

No, it must have sounded like I was gasping.

If I had a functioning vocal cord.

“…”

I reached for my throat.

A strange hissing sound, like smoke.

My gloved hand, gleaming, felt not skin but a rubber-like material, part of the work uniform covering my throat.

I groped. A human silhouette.

But when my hand touched the strange, rib-like flames extending from there to above my head, I dropped my hand.

“…”

I realized again. 

I cannot sleep.

Whirrr!

[It is your work hours. Please wake up. It is your work hours. Please wake up…]

I rose from the metal bed and began today’s work.

As per the contract.
