# Chapter 253

Lee Jaheon opened his eyes.

A neat bedsheet and a refined, clean atmosphere in a two-person dormitory bedroom where gray bricks were exposed.

Outside the window, gray clouds hung dimly, maintaining a gloomy, sunken density.

During the fifteen days he had awakened here, sunlight had never appeared even once.

It must have been a characteristic of this place.

♩ - ♩ -

A low, resonant bell rang out. Lee Jaheon rose from the bed and changed his clothes.

At Saint Anticus Boarding School, it was a school rule to always wear the uniform outside the bedroom.

He arranged the pajamas on the bed.

Every trainee was given pajamas cut from gray linen cloth, and the sizes were divided into five categories. Lee Jaheon neatly folded the largest size of pajamas he had been given.

And those were not the only pajamas that had to be arranged.

Next to the trainee’s pajamas, a white bride’s dress must always be laid out side by side.

This was practice as the groom, and part of the curriculum taught here—“training for a successful wedding.”

Lee Jaheon finished arranging everything in an instant and left the bedroom at the designated time.

♩ - ♩ -

And then breakfast.

One must not be late for mealtime and must eat with proper posture while holding the cutlery in the prescribed order.

The menu differed each time, but today it was almonds, roasted heart, and cornelian cherry juice.

During the meal, students are trained to make sure the bride’s glass is never empty.

The groom’s opposite seat remained empty, and at times the drink in the bride’s glass placed there disappears for no apparent reason.

A failure occurred. Demerit points accumulated.

♩ - ♩ -

In the morning hours, students learned the culture expected of the groom.

They studied and recited poems and idioms related to weddings, learned to play the string instruments preferred by the bride, and practiced etiquette for receiving guests.

Lee Jaheon completed everything flawlessly according to *our* decision, without receiving a single thing to reprimand.

A failure occurred. Demerit points accumulated.

♩ - ♩ -

Lunch consisted of light sandwiches or finger foods, and through the fingers served alongside them, one became accustomed to the bride’s appearance.

So as to become adept when placing the ring during the wedding ceremony.

After finishing the meal, the afternoon was spent going together to the kitchen to prepare the evening’s course meal.

Selecting fresh ingredients, and learning how to cook according to the bride’s taste…

“Ghk—ugh!”

"......"

"......"

“W-wait, no, I—!!”

A failure occurred. Demerit points accumulated.

♩ - ♩ -

The evening course meal continued long and elegantly, and the groom trainees maintained their dignity while eating quietly and cleanly.

Out of consideration for the bride’s eating habits, they separated and left the foods on their plates that they should not eat.

Once all servings are finished, including dessert, they could stand up only after clearing the table and offering thanks to the supervising instructor for the day’s training.

♩ - ♩ -

Then came a short rest period.

Lee Jaheon walked down the corridor at the designated pace.

At this boarding school, for the sake of cleanliness, trainees were advised not to step into places considered unclean or outside the trainee-designated areas, and even the method of rest was specified.

Thus, the most recommended way to spend rest time was to imagine what vows one would speak at the wedding with the bride.

Sometimes they asked about it during breakfast the next morning, so it was best to have an answer prepared…

Someone grabbed his arm in the corridor.

“Hey—hey…!”

Lee Jaheon stopped walking.

A desperate whisper followed him.

“Y-you’re human, right? You are, right…!”

“Yes.”

A pale face, an expression twisted with terror.

A civilian.

“What… what the hell is this place?!”

“This is a boarding school that conducts training to become a good groom, according to the judgment of Saint Anticus.”

“What the hell kind of thing is that in twenty-first century Korea?!”

Lee Jaheon answered.

“It does not exist.”

“H-huh?”

Lee Jaheon moved his foot—

“W-wait! Then… this isn’t Korea? What is this, some cult? Was I kidnapped? This isn’t some fucking horror game… not even a ghost story.”

“That is correct.”

“……What?”

“You are currently trapped inside a strange phenomenon that can be described as a ghost story.”

Lee Jaheon finished his response.

And moved again to carry out the planned task—

“Hey.”

…….

“Then why the hell are you so calm? You must know how to get out. That’s why you’re calm, right?”

“No.”

“What do you mean no? Fuck.”

In the whispering voice, a damp fury with nowhere to go twisted together with desperation as his eyes rolled wildly.

If anyone else had heard it, they would definitely have felt a chilling sensation.

Lee Jaheon did not perceive that complicated atmosphere, but he understood the next words clearly.

“If I’m going to die anyway, want to die together?”

“That is not—”

“Bullshit. If I start a fight with you here, it’s a demerit, you bastard. Want both of us to get demerits? Or want to share how to get out?”

It was a threat.

—During rest time, one must not raise their voice.

He must have been someone who recognized that rule.

No—perhaps that was precisely why he had survived until now….

The problem, however, was that fear had dulled his powers of observation.

“Hey, answer—”

“Do not walk while stomping your feet.”

“…What?”

It was already too late.

A massive shadow fell over the man gripping Lee Jaheon.

The supervising instructor.

“W-wait a second. We’re not fighting—we’re just chatting. It’s rest time. Haha….”

The supervising instructor acknowledged this.

However, since they had behaved in a way that diverged from the school’s educational goals, demerit points would accumulate.

Of course, they were not expelled—only encouraged and assisted.

“…What?”

Because they had walked noisily while stomping their feet in the corridor.

If a groom behaved so inelegantly during a wedding, how shameful that would be. That was why it must be prevented now.

After all, that was why they were receiving instruction at Saint Anticus Boarding School.

“E-excuse me.”

The man was seized.

“Mmmph!”

The supervising instructor moved to assist the one with demerits.

In accordance with the penalty.

“ ~!!! ”

The supervising instructor kindly placed soft rubber and clay into his hands. A noise-dampening pad to shape according to the form of his foot.

He had to complete it himself and insert it into the sole of his foot for the demerit to end.

A gag made of cotton and flesh was inserted into his throat so he could not scream.

Blood from the man writhing in pain and terror stained the corridor.

That too was a demerit.

—Do not cause disturbances during instruction.

Under the supervising instructor’s troubled contemplation, additional measures were carried out.

The trainee in question must walk without producing sound once the foot procedure was complete.

Repeatedly, until he could walk properly without making any noise.

“…!!, !!!”
