# Chapter 232

Ho Yuwon.

A director who likely knows more about Jang Heoun than anyone else in this company.

“You probably have a lot you’re curious about.”

But the untrustworthy figure is smiling at me.

In a corridor inside a ghost story where fire and smoke have turned everything into a skeletal ruin, where corpses lie piled up.

He asks me if I have anything I want to ask.

*'...In that case.'*

There is a way to approach this.

I let the smoke rise.

*Proposal: One-on-One Question and Answer Ritual*

“Hmm?”

*Conditions: Two participants*

*▶ Questions and answers are exchanged alternately in order.*

*▶ The respondent must answer every question truthfully.*

*▶ If a question cannot be answered, the respondent must verbally state “Unable to answer” and then receive the next question.*

*▶ An answer cannot be withheld for more than 96 seconds.*

**[Ah, interesting. You’ve set strangely specific conditions for no reason. The structure even feels a little ritualistic… ]**

I do that on purpose. After all, this is something done with a being that isn’t human.

And…

*'He clearly has questions for me too.'*

If it takes the formal shape of a ritual where questions are exchanged, he cannot help but be tempted.

And.

*▶ If someone intentionally conceals the truth during the ritual, they will receive punishment.*

“…Haha! Are you copying me?”

Yes. Try experiencing the other side for once.

Ho Yuwon laughs several times, then answers easily.

“But I like it.”

Yet a strange glimmer passes through his eyes.

“It’s fair, after all. I could place a prohibition that actually punishes you… but I won’t. I want to trust you, Roe Deer.”

A lie.

He says that because he probably cannot place one easily anyway.

*'He tried to impose a prohibition earlier and got hit by me.'*

But it does not matter.

Agreement

\: One-on-One Question and Answer Ritual

From this moment.

*The ritual begins.*

*Clap.*

“Good. Then… since you proposed it, Roe Deer, I’ll let you ask the first question.”

And so, on Basement Floor 96, at the bizarre fire scene of the Cheerful Research Institute, the two ghost stories begin exchanging questions and answers.

**[*****Friend*****, which truth would you like to know first?]**

The most essential one.

*Question:*

*Everything Ho Yuwon knows about Jang Heoun.*

“You’re asking quite a broad question, Roe Deer.”

Ho Yuwon answers.

“This is what I know. Male, in his 20s, no family. Raised in a religious social welfare facility in the countryside. Three years before he joined the company, a fire destroyed that facility completely. He was the only survivor.”

……

Information that matches the testimony I once heard from the contaminated Mr. Jang Heoun.

“So his wish is probably related to that fire… perhaps reviving the dead, or asking for the fire to be undone.”

Accurate.

*'Did he investigate more closely while selecting him as a spy?'*

“Then it’s my turn to ask a question, Roe Deer.”

Ho Yuwon looks at me.

“If you already knew the story you just heard from me… who did you hear it from?”

…!

“More precisely, what kind of being told you?”

……

*Answer:*

*An employee of Flower Golden Resort — Bison.*

“Ah, I thought so.”

The rekindling sparks cast shadows across **Ho Yuwon’s** face in the backlight.

“I read in the field exploration team’s report that he died in the theme park and revived as a resort employee… but to think you actually heard that directly from him.”

……

“So you didn’t just help Roe Deer revive him… he must be subordinate to you, then?”

*Hoo.*

“Ah, that wasn’t a question. It was a certainty. …As expected, Roe Deer, you’re quite human. You make choices you’ll regret.”

An ominous statement, though the tone carries something almost like regret.

Instead of getting angry, I simply look at Ho Yuwon.

“Anyway, you’re curious about the three "Heoun's" with different surnames, right? I’m curious too, so maybe we can deduce it together through this exchange. …Shall we continue?”

Without wavering, I move on.

*Question:*

*What you know about the new employee Kim Heoun.*

“…Now it starts getting a little interesting.”

Interesting?

“He has the exact same background as Mr. Jang Heoun.”

“…..!!"

Male, in his 20s, no family. Raised in a religious social welfare facility in the countryside. Fire trauma.

“If you say they simply grew up in similar environments, the keywords match *too perfectly*. It’s almost like they were assigned the same role in a game.”

……

Pieces of a puzzle begin to assemble in my mind.

“Then it’s my question. …Did Roe Deer distribute the Wish Tickets you received to the employees of Daydream Inc. who infiltrated the Disaster Management Bureau with you? Exactly who received how many bottles?”

*Answer: Two bottles to employee Goral*

*— Asked her to later deliver them to Employee Bison*

“Ah… just as I thought.”

Wait. *Just as he thought?*

That sounds as if he already knew… could it be—

*Question: Whether the usage of the three Wish Tickets has been tracked.*

“Ah. Rather than tracking them, I simply know.”

…!

“The Wish Tickets I gave you were part of my personal collection. So I applied a little treatment to the bottles… enough to know whether they’ve been used.”

Ho Yuwon smiles.

“And the three Wish Tickets I gave you, Roe Deer, have all been used. …One of them was used by you, right?”

Which means…

The two that went to Go Yeongeun have already been used as well.

A chill runs sharply down my spine.

*'Go Yeongeun used both bottles?'*

Unless someone stole them… no, that seems unlikely.

Go Yeongeun is the type who manages risks carefully.

And she is not the kind of person who would recklessly use Jang Heoun’s Wish Ticket.

Then the most likely possibility is that she used them for Jang Heoun.

*'But…'*

The Jang Heoun I saw has not changed.

“Well, that leads to an interesting guess. …Maybe someone made the wrong wish?”

Ho Yuwon speaks with a tone of pity.

“Half a year passes, and poor Bison shows no sign of recovering from contamination and returning to human form… so in his anxiety, he used it arbitrarily.”

……

“But due to some kind of error, instead of ‘Jang Heoun,’ ‘Kim Heoun’ appeared in this world, and the wish was fulfilled that way.”

A guess that makes my spine turn cold.

**[Hmm. Is that so? Then let’s speculate a bit more specifically.]**

In other words… it means this.

If Go Yeongeun made a wish like *“Please let Mr. Heoun live in this world as a human.”*

*'Then instead of the contaminated Jang Heoun returning to humanity, someone whom Go Yeongeun could accept as ‘Mr. Heoun’ might appear in the world instead…!'*

Wish Tickets are personally omnipotent. So such a ghost-story-like situation is certainly possible. However…

*Skeptical: Judgment reserved*

“Ah?”

There is a puzzle piece that does not fit.

*'The person on this Cheerful Research Institute employee ID.'*

#><**Cheerful Researcher**><#

#><**Lee Heoun**><#

Only this researcher named Lee Heoun remains left over.

This person seems to have existed in the past Cheerful Research Institute, unrelated to the Wish Tickets used now.

*'…There’s definitely something else.'*

Heoun, Heoun, Heoun…

A strange feeling rises.

*'Even the keyword “fire” overlaps.'*

Someone whose surroundings all died in a fire at the facility where he lived.

Looking at the situation on Basement Floor 96, isn’t this Cheerful Research Institute employee the same? Why does he have such a background similar to Jang Heoun… wait.

*'No. It’s the opposite.'*

If I think in chronological order…

*'Jang Heoun and Kim Heoun resemble the background of this Cheerful Research Institute employee…'*

*Rustle.*

……

*Rustle.*

“Roe Deer.”

……

“Did you see that?”

I did.

Just now.

The pile of corpses moved.

No—more precisely, the piles of bodies in front of the elevator only *looked* like they moved.

Because something buried underneath them moved.
