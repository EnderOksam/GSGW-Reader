# New Employee Orientation

6 PM, as the sun began to set.

A dim, golden dusk fell across the entrance of a certain building located in Seoul.

A modern structure with a cold, pristine ground-floor space.

#>< **Daydream Inc. (Ltd.) Annex** ><#

#>< **Entry restricted to authorized personnel only.** ><#

This was the lobby of Daydream Inc.'s annex building.

Yet this sterile, ordinarily quiet and bleak space was currently buzzing with the excited murmuring of people.

"Wow, check out the atmosphere."

"Hello. Yes, nice to meet you...!"

Dozens of young adults in black suits, freshly entering the workforce, had gathered and were greeting each other enthusiastically. Their faces were bright with a mix of anticipation and nervousness.

It was only natural.

"Congratulations on getting hired!"

"Hahaha, thanks! Same to you!"

After all, these were the individuals who had recently succeeded in joining Daydream Inc., the renowned pharmaceutical company that had emerged as a hot topic in the stock market over the past few years.

Thus, the company had summoned them to its annex building in Seoul via email.

New Employee Orientation Notice

Today was the first day of orientation.

"A three-day orientation at the Seoul annex feels a bit luxurious, doesn't it?"

"Absolutely. Wow, this place feels more like an art gallery lobby than anything."

"Yeah, big companies are different after all... Oh, are we all sales team hires?"

"It seems so. Everyone I've talked to is in sales."

"Then maybe other new employees are gathering somewhere else nearby."

The people, intoxicated by their recent hiring success, happily chatted amongst themselves. There was no reason not to be friendly. These were, after all, fellow colleagues they'd continue to see frequently in this pharmaceutical giant. The victorious new hires!

This cheerful atmosphere led some to generously include those who'd remained outside the conversation.

"Excuse me, you're a new hire too, right?"

"......"

Amid the bustling crowd, one individual, who'd been quietly observing the interior of the annex alone, raised his head. A young man around their age, with a weary-looking face.

Feeling somewhat superior, the new employee who initiated the conversation continued addressing him.

"Congratulations on joining the company! Let's work well together!"

However...

"Thank you."

That was it.

"......"

"......"

Silence.

'What the hell?'

How could a new sales employee have such poor social skills?

How did this guy even pass the interview?

As the atmosphere threatened to turn awkward, the person who'd started the conversation quickly chattered again.

"Haha, actually... I have a friend who already works at Daydream."

"Whoa."

"Really?"

"Which department?"

"Yep, yep. I think it was... Research Team or something?"

The crowd responded with explosive interest!

Secretly relieved that he'd saved the atmosphere, the employee kept speaking in a subtly proud tone.

"But you know, he was weirdly discouraging me from joining. He said I'd regret it if I worked here, or something like that."

"Haha!"

"Friends who got jobs earlier always say stuff like that."

"Exactly!"

"......"

People laughed as if it were typical office-worker banter about being overworked.

But...

"...epartment."

"Hm?"

The quiet one opened his mouth again.

"That friend of yours, which department was---"

At that very moment.

*[Ah, ah--- Mic test.]*

"...!"

Everyone raised their heads simultaneously.

*[Aigoo, everyone gathered well. Then let's begin~]*

A speaker mounted at the corner of the wall emitted a lively, easygoing voice.

A man's casual voice continued.

*[Welcome, new hires who made it through the competition of 142 to 1! I'm Kwak Jekang, the Section Chief from Research Team 1, who'll be leading your orientation today.]*

*[Shall we start with a round of applause?]*

*Woooaahh!*

The new hires enthusiastically clapped, though slightly confused.

"Why use a speaker...?"

Why wasn't he appearing in person?

"Hm, maybe it's something we're supposed to do on our own?"

*[Haha. Correct answer!]*

"...!"

'H-He was listening...!'

*[Exactly, exactly. From now on, to build teamwork and company spirit, you new hires will engage in a very entertaining activity~]*

*[Well, it's just one light mission, consider it a recreational activity. There'll even be prizes!]*

The new employees quickly took this positively.

"I guess this is to help us quickly bond with each other!"

"That actually sounds great, haha."

After all, many large companies and prominent venture firms nowadays conducted their orientation sessions creatively or uniquely.

"I heard one company even uses their office for a murder-mystery board game."

*[Aha, wow, that does sound fun! This one's similar, you see. Yep, indeed~ Now now, then...]*

The speaker's voice grew excited, its tone rising.

*[Let's begin the New Employee Orientation right now!]*

At the same instant.

*Tadadadadak.*

Lights switched on in the annex entrance.

"...!"

Under the bright lights, things previously unseen began to appear clearly.

Down the corridor, previously obscured by darkness, stood rows of heavy iron doors.

"......"

Somehow, rather than maintaining security, it felt more like something was being cut off and locked away... The atmosphere was heavy and alien.

'Why would there be something like that in the company annex...?'

But before anyone could linger on their doubts, everyone's gaze was drawn to the spot most brightly illuminated by the overhead lights.

There, on the front desk.

An object that stood out.

"Ah...!"

The new employees blinked as they spotted it.

A jet-black cylindrical box.

"A lottery box?"

It was large and old.

Its battered, crumpled appearance was out of place in the modern, pristine marble lobby of the annex.

The thick paper cylinder was covered on top so its contents were hidden, but the silhouette of the lid revealed there were three holes.

Each round lid was numbered.

#>< #r⓵   ⓶   ⓷r# ><#

*[Alright, step forward one at a time and draw a lot. Pick the next number in order, and then keep going that way.]*

*[Ah, let me show you how it's done. You three who are standing closest, come draw a number, one at a time!]*

"Uh, yessir!"

Though they hesitated a bit, the three that were called out couldn't refuse and stepped forward.

And drew lots.

*Shuffle.*

The slips each had something written on them.

#>< #r⓵ 6 BFr# ><#

#>< #r⓶ 322r# ><#

#>< #r⓷ Recite a nursery rhymer# ><#

"What..."

*[Now, look at the desk again. You see the whiteboard behind the lottery box, right?]*

Everyone's attention turned to the whiteboard.

Printed sentences were displayed.

#>< After going down to #r⓵r#\_\_\_ ><#

#>< and reaching room number #r⓶r#\_\_\_, ><#

#>< I will #r⓷r#\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_. ><#

[If you fill in the blanks on the whiteboard with what you drew, the sentence is complete!]

[And the mission is to carry out exactly what that sentence says. Too easy, right? Haha!]

They filled in the numbers on the whiteboard, and the sentence was completed.

#>< After going down to #*#r6r#*# BF ><#

#>< and reaching room numbe #*#r322r#*#, ><#

#>< I will #* #rrecite a nursery rhymer#*#. ><#

"......"

"......"

For some reason, the completed sentence gave off a strange, unsettling feeling.

'Basement?'

At that moment.

*[Ah, and of course, there's an amazing prize waiting for the team with the best mission performance.]*

The speaker's cheerful motivational tone seemed to pick up on the room's mood.

*[You'll get 100 points, usable just like cash in the employee mall!]*

A hundred points?

"May I ask what we can buy with that?"

*[Of course! It's enough to buy a robot vacuum cleaner!]*

"Oooh!"

If you could get a robot vacuum cleaner, it must be quite a sum!

The atmosphere brightened noticeably.

Rationality and realism returned to their calculations.

'Worst case, it's just a test of courage, right?'

'There's no way the company would have us do anything genuinely dangerous.'

Besides, with so many people here and teams of three, there was hardly any room to be scared.

Even if there were some surprise, it had to be something even those who were easily frightened could endure.

The new hires exchanged nods and smiles.

"Excuse me, do we have to take the stairs?"

*[Oh, wouldn't that be too much trouble? You can use any of the currently operating elevators to move. Hahaha!]*

*[Now, go ahead and form your teams however you like!]*

"Yes!"

They talked among themselves, faces flushed with excitement, and reached their hands into the black box to draw their fate.

"Let's do this!"

"We three will go first. We just take this elevator, right?"

"Let's go. Basement 6!"

The mood was better than ever.

At least, until thirty-four minutes later, when the elevator doors opened again and revealed only the lower halves of their colleagues.

"Aaaaah!"

"Aaagh!"

And it continued.

*Ding.*

*Ding.*

*Ding.*

Each time the elevator arrived and its doors opened, screams split the air and echoed through the annex lobby.

Screams. Terror. Panic.

The elevators, arriving one after another, were filled with 'what used to be bodies,' each in a different state.

Horrors no one would have imagined encountering in real life. Blood, filth, pieces of viscera, sometimes ash or ice, and even scraps of skin covered with dense writing in pen.

There were even employees who, using their own blood, scrawled bizarre phrases or patterns on the elevator mirrors before dying of blood loss.

"Uuaaagh!!!"

Those who managed to return alive were in no condition to speak.

"We have to run! Run! Run!"

"I-I'm sorry. I'm sorry for knocking on the door, I'm so sorry..."

Some mumbled in a daze.

Then, finally, a few who were still lucid appeared.

"Th, This person is okay!"

"......"

"What on earth happened?! What is this?!"

A new hire, using the corpse of a colleague as a shield in front of the elevator doors, spoke with a deathly pale face.

"There's someone there."

Down in the basement where they'd gone, behind the door---

"Someone? Who?! Who's there?!"

......

"A... A strange employee."

And then survivors' accounts came pouring out.

When the elevator doors opened, what was revealed was a cold, desolate corridor in the basement.

The many iron doors there, and the strange markings found only on the door they were assigned to. The sounds, the smell of blood, the light that came from just that door...

And the scene glimpsed through the small hole above the room number plate.

An employee.

"They were wearing an employee uniform... but it was weird. Really weird..."

A child in a lab coat. One with an oxygen tank. One holding a bunch of balloons. One with a cowboy hat and wooden hands. One wearing a lion dance mask...

The descriptions of those bizarre appearances made everyone's blood run cold.

And then---

"I-It responded."

"...!"

"It reacted to what we did! It ch-chased after us, and then... like that!"

Heads turned toward the elevator doors, now filled with body parts.

"......"

"......"

The new hires realized.

This 'do as the sentence says' mission.

It was basically a suicide act straight out of a horror movie.

They had been swept up into an unreal catastrophe.

"No!"

About half of the new hires, those who hadn't yet gone down to do their mission, looked at each other in terror, then bolted for the main entrance.

But the annex's only door was already sealed.

People clung to the door where the shutter had come down.

"Please let us out!"

"M-My phone doesn't work. What do we do?"

"This... This has to be some kind of prank show. Y-Yeah, there must be cameras somewhere..."

"Please open the door! Please, I'm begging you!"

*Bang, bang, bang!*

Even when they pounded on the door until their fists bled, there was no response.

A few new hires caught on to another method of communication.

"The speakers!"

They all rushed over to the speakers that had broadcast 'Section Chief Kwak Jekang's' voice, begging for help, pleading in fear.

But...

*[(Beep-) No attendant available. Automated response system activated.]*

Section Chief Kwak Jekang's voice could no longer be heard from the speakers.

Instead, a recorded, cheerful voice played on loop.

*[The main entrance will open once all participants have attempted the mission. In 30 seconds, any attempt to force the shutter will result in death by security protocol.]*

*[Orientation participants, keep your spirits up!]*

*Bee-bee-bee-beep--- beep!*

"Aaaagh!"

Two employees who tried to break the shutter had their heads burst open.

And then, they died.

Pandemonium.

"U-Uhhhhh..."

"Hicc..."

No one could understand how or why this was happening.

Faced with something so impossible, so unreal, the chaos in the lobby only gradually died down.

From then on, the people in the lobby gathered trembling before the front desk, clinging to each other for more than half a day.

Occasionally, there was whispered discussion and some movement to try to find a way out, but at least, nobody so much as approached the elevators.

No one seemed willing to complete the mission.

Then came a brutal incentive.

*[Mission delay has exceeded six hours. This is an announcement.]*

*[If the next participant does not board the elevator within five minutes, death by security protocol will occur.]*

"......"

"......"

The new hires looked at each other with trembling eyes.

Someone.

Someone had to go.

Someone *had* to get on the elevator.

Who would it be?

"Fuck..."

The new hire who, hours earlier, had boasted about having a friend at the company, now tried to hide in a corner, avoiding everyone's gaze.

Sweating coldly, he looked again at the sentence his team had completed.

#>< After going down to #*#r13r#*# BF ><#

#>< and reaching room numbe #*#r666r#*#, ><#

#>< I will #*#rknock and go insider#*#. ><#

He's going to die.

There was no way he'd survive with numbers that ominous.

'Run...'

Then.

"The next ones up! The ones who already drew!"

Damn it!

"There they are! It's those people!"

"...!"

The others rushed over and forcibly shoved his team into the elevator.

They struggled like mad, but were outnumbered, with no weapons or means to resist.

"No!"

*[The doors are closing.]*

As he tried to get up, struggling in panic, he desperately pressed the open button.

At that moment, one of his teammates grabbed his hand and pulled it down.

"...!"

"It's too late."

*Thunk.*

The doors closed.

"Hiiek!"

In a terror so deep he couldn't even feel anger, he turned his head and saw the pale-faced peer staring at him.

The one who'd always been weirdly quiet.

But now, that person spoke calmly, almost as if explaining.

"Because of the security protocol, someone died. Which means, even if we just stayed in the lobby like this, we'd die all the same..."

"......"

"At this point, we have no choice but to go through with it."

"N-No...!"

"Calm down and think. There were quite a few people who came back unharmed."

As if to snap them out of it, the other person grabbed that guy's shoulder.

"...At least one out of every three teams testified that 'nothing happened' to them."

"...!!"

It was true.

*-- Nothing really happened...*

*-- Just... there was a weird person in some kind of special agent uniform silently staring at us.*

*-- There was really no one inside!*

"For the teams where something did happen, about half of them still had survivors. So, compared to just dying in the lobby, your odds are better this way."

"......"

"...Let's move carefully, based on the previous teams' testimonies."

Gasping for breath, the two new hires slowly nodded.

It was thanks to the composure of the one who seemed almost like an experienced veteran.

The new hire who had tried to act self-important ended up speaking without realizing it.

"Um, by the way, may I ask your name...?"

"......"

The previously silent person spoke up.

"My name is Jang Heoun."

*Ding.*

The elevator arrived at the basement.

Shaking, the new hires stepped out into the old hallway lined with iron doors.

And they found the room they were looking for.
